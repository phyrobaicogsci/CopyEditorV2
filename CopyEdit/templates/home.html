Hey! I am CopyEditor.



Here are some rules I follow:

  • If you find a capital in the middle of your sentence, unless it's a proper noun, it shouldn't be there.
  • The names of subjects, like math, should not be capitalized unless they are at the beginning of a sentence.
  • The dollar sign '$' precedes the numerical value, without any spaces between them.Forexample, $2.
  • The cent sign ‘¢’ comes after the number value, without any spaces. For example, 2¢.
  • There should be a space on either side of the minus sign '-'. For example, a - b.
  • There should be a space on either side of the plus sign '+'. For example, a + b.
  • There should be a space on either side of the multiplication sign 'x'. For example, a x b.
  • There shouldn't be a space on either side of a slash '/'. For example, a/b.
  • There should be a space on either side of the equal sign '='. For example, a = b
  • There should not be a space on either side of a ratio ':'. For example, a:b.
  • There should be no space before a colon and one space after it. For example, the number of balloons required: 5 dozen
  • The aforementioned rules apply within parentheses '()'.
  • Please refrain from adding extra exclamation marks (!) or question marks (?) for emphasis, as this is not appropriate for the text.
  • Be careful with using American English especially in the case of units of measurement. For example, kilometer, not kilometre.
  • Be consistent in the abbreviations for units of measurement. For example, it is km/h not kms/hr.
  • Use oxford commas. For example, I have a pen, paper and pencil. I have a pen, paper, and pencil.
  • When writing fractions, hyphenate the two words. For example, two-fourths.
  • Do not add full stops at the end of equations.
  • Everything except buttons on wireframes must be written in sentence case.
  • Buttons must be uppercase (on wireframes).Buttons must be uppercase (on wireframes).
